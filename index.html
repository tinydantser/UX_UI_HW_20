The Challenge

Research, prototype, test, and validate an idea for a brand-new mobile application. The application must solve a clearly identified need in users' everyday lives.

The Idea

Create an application that could help home cooks reduce the amount of food that goes to waste.

The Conclusion

Pay more attention to what the users are telling you! Our user research uncovered a goldmine of data and had we more time to analyze the data properly we would have seen that at the time.

The average amount of money wasted on spoiled food was estimated at $10 a week. Some responses reached as high as $20-25 a week!

Prototype

Using iOS Human Interface Guidelines we created high fidelity mockups.

Home Screen
  • Easily visual the spaces in your kitchen.
  • Immediate reprsentation of the "freshness" status of each space.
  • Not satisfied with our space defaults? Create one yourself!
List of all the food in your kitchen
  • Making dinner? Easily find all the food in your kitchen.
  • Double check for food in your kitchen before making purchases at the grocery store.
  • Easily check on the "freshness" status of each item in your kitchen.
Alternative ideas to throwing food in the garbage
  • If a food item has expired, we will offer you creative solutions to use it.
  • Some recipes like banana bread can still be made with overripe bananas.

Conclusions

We gathered enough data in the research phase to create three or four more features to this application.
I would love to spend more time working on Waste Smart to fully flesh out those ideas and bring them to life.

So if you or anyone you know is trying to reduce the waste in their kitchen, look out for Waste Smart because you never know!
